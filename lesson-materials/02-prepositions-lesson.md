## 📋 LESSON OBJECTIVES

By the end of this lesson, the student will be able to:
- [ ] Use AT, ON, IN correctly for time expressions
- [ ] Use AT, ON, IN correctly for place expressions
- [ ] Distinguish between FOR and SINCE
- [ ] Use common dependent prepositions with verbs and adjectives
- [ ] Identify and correct preposition errors

### Special Cases - NO PREPOSITION! ⚠️

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| this/that/next/last + time | *I saw him **last week**. (NOT: in last week)* |
| every + time | *She calls **every day**. (NOT: in every day)* |
| today, tomorrow, yesterday | *I'll see you **tomorrow**. (NOT: on tomorrow)* |

### FOR vs SINCE - The Critical Difference

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                           FOR vs SINCE                                       ║
╠══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║                                                                              ║
║   FOR ────► DURATION (How long?)                                            ║
║   │                                                                          ║
║   │    ◄────────── a period of time ──────────►                             ║
║   │                                                                          ║
║   │    • for two hours      • for a week        • for 10 years              ║
║   │    • for a long time    • for ages          • for centuries             ║
║   │                                                                          ║
║   │    "I have lived here FOR five years."                                  ║
║   │                        └─────────────┘                                   ║
║   │                         (5 years = duration)                            ║
║   │                                                                          ║
║   SINCE ──► STARTING POINT (When did it start?)                             ║
║   │                                                                          ║
║   │         │                                                                ║
║   │    start point ─────────────────────────────► now                       ║
║   │                                                                          ║
║   │    • since 2010         • since Monday      • since I was a child       ║
║   │    • since last month   • since then        • since we met              ║
║   │                                                                          ║
║   │    "I have lived here SINCE 2019."                                      ║
║   │                        └─────────┘                                       ║
║   │                         (2019 = starting point)                         ║
║   │                                                                          ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

⚠️ COMMON MISTAKE:
   ✗ "I have lived here since five years."
   ✓ "I have lived here FOR five years."
   ✓ "I have lived here SINCE 2019."
```

```

║                           BY vs UNTIL                                        ║
╠══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║                                                                              ║
║   BY ────► DEADLINE (at or before this time)                                ║
║   │                                                                          ║
║   │    ──────────────────────── │ deadline                                  ║
║   │    action completed here ───►│                                          ║
║   │                                                                          ║

║   │    (= on Friday or any time before Friday)                              ║
║   │                                                                          ║
║   UNTIL/TILL ──► UP TO A POINT (continuous until then)                      ║

║   │    action continues here ───►│                                          ║
║   │                                                                          ║
║   │    "I will work UNTIL 6 PM."                                            ║
║   │    (= continuously from now to 6 PM)                                    ║
║   │                                                                          ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

### Visual: AT vs IN vs ON

```
                    AT                    IN                     ON
                    
               [specific point]     [inside/enclosed]      [surface/line]
               
                    📍                   📦                    ═══
                   here               [inside]               on top

Examples:       at the door          in the box            on the table
                at the station       in the room           on the wall
                at the corner        in the car            on the bus
```

## ✍️ PART 4: Exercises (20 minutes)

### Exercise 1: Time Prepositions - Fill in AT, ON, IN, or ∅ (no preposition)

1. I wake up _______ 7 o'clock _______ the morning.
2. The meeting is _______ Monday _______ 3 PM.
3. I was born _______ 1995 _______ March.
4. She arrived _______ night _______ last Friday.
5. We're going on holiday _______ next week.
6. The shop is closed _______ Sundays.
7. I'll see you _______ two weeks.
8. What are you doing _______ the weekend?
9. He finished the project _______ the end of the month.
10. _______ Christmas Day, we always have a big dinner.

---

### Exercise 1: ANSWER KEY

1. I wake up **at** 7 o'clock **in** the morning.
2. The meeting is **on** Monday **at** 3 PM.
3. I was born **in** 1995 **in** March.
4. She arrived **at** night **∅** last Friday.
5. We're going on holiday **∅** next week.
6. The shop is closed **on** Sundays.
7. I'll see you **in** two weeks.
8. What are you doing **at/on** the weekend?
9. He finished the project **at** the end of the month.
10. **On** Christmas Day, we always have a big dinner.

   > _______________________________________________

    > _______________________________________________

### Exercise 5: ANSWER KEY

1. I've been living here **for** three years. *(duration = FOR)*
2. She's very good **at** mathematics. *(good AT)*
3. The meeting will start **at** 3 PM. *(specific time = AT)*
4. I'm waiting **for** the bus. *(wait FOR)*
5. He went **∅** home early. *(no preposition with "home")*
6. This is different **from** what I expected. *(different FROM)*
7. She's married **to** a doctor. *(married TO)*
8. I'm interested **in** this job. *(interested IN)*
9. It depends **on** the situation. *(depend ON)*
10. He apologized **for** his mistake. *(apologize FOR)*
11. I arrived **at** the airport at 6 PM. *(arrive AT a place)*
12. She's afraid **of** dogs. *(afraid OF)*
13. The book is **on** the table. *(surface = ON)*
14. I'll see you **on** Monday. *(day = ON)*
15. He insisted **on** helping me. *(insist ON)*

## 🗣️ PART 5: Speaking Activity (10 minutes)

### Activity: "Describe Your Day"

**Instructions:** Describe your typical day using as many different prepositions as possible.

**Prompts to include:**
- What time do you wake up? (AT)
- What day do you work/study? (ON)
- What month were you born? (IN)
- How long have you lived in your current home? (FOR/SINCE)
- Where do you usually eat lunch? (AT/IN)
- What are you interested in? (IN)
- What are you good at? (AT)
- What do you often think about? (ABOUT)

**Example:**
> "I usually wake up **at** 7 o'clock **in** the morning. I work **on** weekdays and rest **on** weekends. I've been living in my apartment **for** two years, **since** 2022. I usually eat lunch **at** a café near my office. I'm interested **in** technology and I'm quite good **at** using computers. I often think **about** my future career."

### Teacher Notes:
- Listen for correct preposition usage
- Note common errors for correction
- Encourage self-correction

| since three years | for three years | Duration = FOR |
| good in | good at | good AT |
| interested for | interested in | interested IN |
| married with | married to | married TO |
| depend of | depend on | depend ON |
| different than | different from | different FROM |
| arrive to | arrive at/in | arrive AT (point) / IN (city) |
| in Monday | on Monday | Days = ON |
| in 3 PM | at 3 PM | Time = AT |
| wait the bus | wait for the bus | wait FOR |
